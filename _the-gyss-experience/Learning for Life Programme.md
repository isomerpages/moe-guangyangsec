<p>Our school's Learn for Life Programme (LLP) is "Guangyang Advocates".
We aim to nurture students who actively lead positive change in the school
and wider community by building Emerging 21st Century Competencies (E21CC),
particularly collaboration, communication skills, and civic literacy, alongside
values of empathy and service. "Guangyang Advocates" develops students
holistically through a two-tiered spiral approach where students discover
strengths through authentic leadership experiences and engaging in Service
Learning projects that develop their sense of purpose and agency. These
experiences directly support our desired student outcomes of nurturing
Leaders who build connections and lead with empathy, and Advocates who
demonstrate strong civic consciousness and actively improve the lives of
others.</p>
<h3><strong>Key Programmes</strong></h3>
<p><strong><u>Leadership Workshops</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Students develop leadership competencies and civic literacy through Lower
and Upper Secondary Leadership Workshops that spiral progressively from
Personal Leadership and Action for Community Improvement to Team Leadership
and Awareness and Advocacy for Community Improvement. Values pertinent
to service-learning and community leadership, particularly empathy and
service, are emphasised in both lower and upper secondary workshops.</p>

<p><strong><u>Service Learning Day</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Leadership competencies and civic literacy are also developed through
the Annual Service Learning Day for the whole school, with level-specific
activities that increase in complexity and impact. It serves as a platform
for students to practise competencies and skills learned at the Leadership
Workshops, as well as demonstrate values and serve the community.</p>
